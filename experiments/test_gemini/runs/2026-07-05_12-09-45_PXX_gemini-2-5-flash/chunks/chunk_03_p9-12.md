## Classification criteria you actually used
- **Assumption vs. Context:** "Context" described the factual setup (e.g., specific optimizer, GPU, dataset, model size, or general characteristics of the ARC task). "Assumption" would have been a precondition for a claim's validity, but no explicit, unhedged assumptions were found. For instance, "ARC tasks are unambiguous" was classified as context because it describes a general characteristic of the domain, not a strict precondition for a specific mechanism to hold.
- **Mechanism vs. Pattern:** "Mechanism" explained *why* something happened or *how* a process works (e.g., "to encourage the model to focus on foreground pixels," "diversity of training tasks is helpful for generalization"). "Pattern" described *what* happened to the numbers or observations (e.g., "accuracy increases," "returns diminish," "scores are zero").
- **Intervention (implicit vs. absent):** Interventions were identified as explicit actions taken by the authors (e.g., "we apply 2 flippings," "we train for 100 epochs," "we visualize"). If a claim was made without a clear, active manipulation by the authors, the intervention was considered absent for that specific claim, but I ensured that related claims from the same experiment were linked to a common intervention if one existed.
- **Eval_metric vs. Pattern:** "Eval_metric" was the name or definition of the measurement (e.g., "Evaluation Accuracy (%)", "pass@k accuracy"). "Pattern" was the observed trend or specific numerical result using that metric (e.g., "accuracy increases," "achieves 31.5%"). These were frequently split from the same sentence.
- **Rule of thumb:** I prioritized splitting sentences into the smallest possible clauses that fulfilled a single role. This was crucial for sentences that combined an action, a result, and an explanation.

- **References:** The entire "References" section (pages 1-2) was excluded as it contains external citations, not the paper's own content.
- **Section/Subsection Headers:** These were skipped as they are organizational and do not convey specific claims, mechanisms, or experimental details.
- **Figure/Table Labels without content:** Labels like "60", "50", "0", "10" on axes were skipped as they are just numerical labels, not descriptive text.
- **Figure/Table References:** Phrases like "See Fig. 14" or "This is illustrated in Fig. 13" were skipped as they are pointers, not content.
- **Hedged statements:** No clauses were classified as "assumption" because none met the strict certainty gate. For example, "some may admit multiple plausible explanations" (S144) was not extracted as an assumption due to the hedging word "may," but the descriptive fact about ARC tasks was extracted as context.
