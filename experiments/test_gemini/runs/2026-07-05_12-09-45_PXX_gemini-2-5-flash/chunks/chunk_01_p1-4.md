## Classification criteria you actually used
For this paper, the distinction between "assumption" and "context" was primarily based on whether the statement implied a condition that *must* hold for a claim to be valid, or if it was merely a descriptive fact about the setup. For example, "colors in ARC do not correspond to real-world colors" (A2) is an assumption because it directly explains *why* certain interpolations are not meaningful. In contrast, "ARC consists of a collection of many different tasks" (C8) is a descriptive fact.

"Mechanism" was identified by looking for causal explanations, often signaled by phrases like "to incorporate," "from which they learn," "enabling it to," or "which helps." It explains *why* an intervention leads to an effect. A plain description of "what happened" (e.g., "VARC achieves 54.5% accuracy") was classified as "pattern."

Interventions were generally explicit actions taken by the authors, such as "we formulate ARC," "we represent inputs," or "we train our model." If an action was clearly implied by the description of a method (e.g., "we perform data augmentations" from "Formally, we perform:"), it was still extracted.

"Eval_metric" captured the specific measurement method or unit (e.g., "accuracy," "per-pixel cross-entropy loss"). "Pattern" captured the quantitative or qualitative trend observed using that metric (e.g., "achieves 60.4% accuracy," "substantially surpasses"). When both appeared in the same sentence, they were always split into separate nodes.

## What you deliberately did NOT extract
I deliberately excluded clauses containing hedging language (e.g., "may," "might," "could") when evaluating for "assumption." For instance, if a sentence stated "results may not hold on Y," the "may not hold" part was discarded at Step 5's certainty gate, even if "we tested only on X" was extracted as context. This ensured that only definite preconditions were labeled as assumptions. I also avoided extracting general statements of purpose or high-level goals that didn't describe a specific causal link or experimental setup. For example, "We hope our research will shed light on the ARC problem" (S42) was not extracted as it's a statement of aspiration, not a testable claim or mechanism.
