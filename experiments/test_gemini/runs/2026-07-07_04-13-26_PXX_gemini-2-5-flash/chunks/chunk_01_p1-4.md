## Classification criteria you actually used
- **Assumption vs. Context**: "Assumption" was reserved for explicit conditions or requirements that, if violated, would invalidate a claim or method's success (e.g., "the model is expected to make predictions on unseen tasks"). "Context" described factual background information about the benchmark, models, or existing methods, without implying a failure condition if changed (e.g., "ARC consists of puzzle-like tasks").
- **Mechanism vs. What happened**: "Mechanism" explained *why* an intervention led to an effect or *how* a component achieved its purpose (e.g., "canvas helps reduce overfitting and encourages learning spatial priors"). "What happened" was captured by "pattern" (the observed trend) or "context" (a descriptive fact about the setup).
- **Implicit vs. Absent Intervention**: An intervention was considered implicit if the text described a specific action or design choice made by the authors to achieve a stated goal, even if not explicitly phrased as "we intervened by X". If a claim was made without any described action or manipulation by the authors, the intervention was null. For this paper, most interventions were explicit or clearly implied by the methodological descriptions.
- **Eval_metric vs. Pattern**: "Eval_metric" captured the name or definition of the measurement instrument (e.g., "accuracy", "per-pixel cross-entropy loss"). "Pattern" captured the observed trend or shape of the results (e.g., "achieves 60.4%", "substantially surpasses"). Numeric values were always moved from "pattern" text to its "note" field.
- **Rule of thumb**: I consistently split sentences that contained both a description of an action/setup and its purpose/effect into separate "intervention" and "mechanism" nodes, or "context" and "pattern" nodes, to ensure minimal span text and single labels. Figures and their captions were treated as primary sources for interventions, contexts, and patterns.

## What you deliberately did NOT extract
- **Hedged statements**: Clauses containing hedging language like "may involve" (S18) were discarded at Step 5's certainty gate for assumptions. For example, "Representative methods may involve inductive reasoning" was not extracted as an assumption because of "may involve".
- **General statements of intent/contribution**: Phrases like "We hope our research will shed light..." (S47) or "We invite the vision community to study..." (S52) were not extracted as they are high-level statements of purpose rather than specific AIO-nodes.
- **Section headers**: These were used for S-numbering but not extracted as nodes themselves.
- **References**: While references were part of the `source_span`, the bracketed numbers themselves (e.g., `[12]`) were not extracted as separate nodes unless they were part of a specific metric or context definition.
