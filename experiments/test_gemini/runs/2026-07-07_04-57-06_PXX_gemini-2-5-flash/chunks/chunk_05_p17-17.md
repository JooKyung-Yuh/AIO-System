- **Mechanism vs. What happened:** "Mechanism" explained *how* or *why* something occurred, such as the model "adapting to task-specific transformations" or improving "by refining color and spatial arrangement." "What happened" was captured by "pattern," describing the observed trends like "gradually converge" or "produces coarse structures."
- **Implicit vs. Absent Intervention:** Interventions were identified from explicit mentions of processes or manipulations ("test-time training process," "test-time training progresses," "few-shot test-time training"). The visual elements of the figure, like the "TTT process" panels, also strongly implied the intervention.

"Pattern" described the observed trend or characteristic of the results, like "gradually converge" or "produces coarse and imprecise structures." I ensured no numeric magnitudes were in the pattern text.
- **Rule of thumb:** For figures, I treated the labels within the figure (e.g., "Input", "TTT process", "Ground truth") and the visual progression itself as primary sources for interventions, contexts, and patterns, in addition to the caption text.
